BlendME: A VR Blendshape Microexpression Dataset with Retrospective Self-Annotation

The first microexpression dataset captured via HMD face tracking, natively in blendshape format.

Abstract

VR-native microexpression capture at scale

Microexpressions (MEs) are brief (40–500 ms), involuntary facial movements that reveal suppressed emotions. Traditional capture requires high-speed cameras, expert FACS annotation, and tightly controlled laboratory conditions. We present BlendME, the first VR-native microexpression dataset captured using Meta Quest Pro face tracking — recording 70 blendshape activations at 45 Hz from 30 participants under a suppression paradigm.

We introduce a Temporal Residual Microactivation (TRM) pipeline that recovers ME-frequency signals attenuated by the headset's internal smoothing, and a retrospective self-annotation interface with avatar replay that replaces expert FACS coding. After quality filtering, the dataset yields 112 high-confidence ME clips from 21 participants. Cross-dataset FACS AU validation against CASME II, SAMM, and SMIC confirms emotion-specific correspondence, with within-dataset separability (d′ = 0.96) exceeding established camera-based benchmarks (d′ = 0.72).

Data collection pipeline

Participants wore a Meta Quest Pro headset and viewed 14 validated emotion-elicitation stimuli under a suppression paradigm. Blendshape time series were captured continuously at 45 Hz alongside head pose, confidence scores, and phase labels. Post-session, participants annotated detected activation peaks via an avatar-replay interface.

Figure 1. End-to-end data collection pipeline under suppression paradigm: onboarding, VR experiment session (calibration, neutral baseline, 14 stimulus trials with in-session self-report), and post-session retrospective annotation.

Retrospective annotation interface

Participants review TRM-detected peaks alongside the stimulus video and an avatar replaying their recorded blendshape activations, assigning emotion labels and intensity ratings.

Method

Temporal Residual Microactivation (TRM)

The Meta Quest Pro applies internal smoothing that attenuates brief (40–500 ms) facial movements. TRM recovers these signals by analyzing how rapidly blendshape values change rather than their raw amplitudes, operating independently on each of the 63 facial channels.

1
Baseline subtraction
Subtract per-participant resting state from every frame to produce a deviation signal d(t).
2
Temporal derivatives
Compute velocity, acceleration, and jerk. MEs produce sharp spikes in acceleration due to rapid onset (< 100 ms).
3
Bandpass filtering
Butterworth filter isolates 2–25 Hz ME-frequency range from velocity signal, removing slow expressions and sensor noise.
4
Temporal energy
RMS over a 150 ms sliding window measures sustained activity rather than momentary spikes.
5
Event detection
Adaptive threshold at 2 SD above mean energy per channel. Events gated to 40–500 ms duration.
